# Atlanta Transportation System Analysis & Improvements

## Current State Analysis

### Major Highway System

**Interstate System:**
- **I-285 ("The Perimeter")**: 64-mile loop encircling Atlanta, 8-12 lanes wide (up to 18 lanes at Spaghetti Junction)
- **I-75**: North-south route through metro Atlanta
- **I-85**: Northeast-southwest route through metro Atlanta  
- **Downtown Connector**: I-75/I-85 concurrent section (12-14 lanes), carries 437,000 vehicles/day at busiest point
- **I-20**: East-west route crossing through Atlanta

**Current Problems:**
1. <cite index="11-7">More than two million people use I-285 each day</cite>
2. <cite index="15-8,15-9">I-285 had 3.5 fatal crashes per 10 miles in 2013</cite>
3. <cite index="31-12">I-285 at I-85 North (#4) and I-20 at I-285 West (#5) are among the worst truck bottlenecks in the US</cite>
4. <cite index="36-6,36-7">The average Atlantan sits in traffic 65 hours each year, making Atlanta the 8th worst congestion in the country</cite>

### Current MARTA Rail System

<cite index="4-11,4-12,4-13">MARTA has four service lines: Red, Gold, Blue, and Green. The Red and Gold lines run along the North-Northeast corridor, and the Blue and Green Lines run along the West-East corridor. The two corridors connect at Five Points station, the only station where transfers are possible between all four lines.</cite>

## Equity and Community Protection Framework

### Historical Context

Atlanta's highway system has a legacy of harm to Black communities:
- **I-20 and I-75/85** were built through thriving Black neighborhoods in the 1950s-60s
- Destroyed communities like Sweet Auburn, Buttermilk Bottom, and Summerhill
- Physically separated neighborhoods and displaced thousands of residents
- Created barriers that persist today

### Core Principles for Equitable Improvements

**1. NO NEW HIGHWAYS through existing residential neighborhoods**
- Outer perimeter MUST use industrial corridors and undeveloped land
- Avoid eminent domain takings of homes and churches
- Preserve community integrity

**2. PRIORITIZE TRANSIT over highway expansion in populated areas**
- Transit connects communities; highways divide them
- Focus on elevated or underground solutions where necessary
- Use existing rights-of-way (rail corridors, utility easements)

**3. INVEST FIRST in underserved communities**
- Southside Atlanta (predominantly Black, lower-income) gets priority
- Murphy Crossing BeltLine connection serves Southwest Atlanta first
- Extend transit to Clayton County (51% Black, median income $56K)

**4. ANTI-DISPLACEMENT protections**
- Community land trusts to preserve affordable housing near new stations
- Right of return guarantees for any displaced residents
- Local hiring requirements (60% from affected communities)
- Small business protection and relocation assistance

**5. COMMUNITY GOVERNANCE**
- Community boards with veto power over designs affecting their neighborhoods
- Majority representation from impacted residents
- Transparent decision-making with public comment periods

## What We're NOT Doing (Anti-Displacement Commitments)

❌ **NO highway expansion through residential neighborhoods**
- No widening of I-75/85 Downtown Connector through Black communities
- No new interchanges that would require demolition
- Outer loop ONLY if zero residential impact

❌ **NO transit-induced displacement without protection**
- Every new station includes community land trust
- 40% affordable housing requirement within 1/2 mile
- Right of return for any displaced residents
- Rent stabilization programs

❌ **NO "urban renewal" schemes disguised as transit**
- Community veto power over development plans
- Majority-Black community boards for oversight
- Transparent procurement with local business preference

❌ **NO suburban bias in funding allocation**
- Southside and Clayton County get 60% of Phase 1 funding
- Suburban extensions only after equity priorities met
- No express lanes that benefit wealthy commuters first

## Conclusion

Atlanta's transportation challenges stem from:
- Over-reliance on highway system with insufficient capacity
- Limited geographic coverage of MARTA rail system
- **Historic pattern of highways destroying Black communities**
- **Racist exclusion of Black suburbs from transit access**
- Suburban sprawl without corresponding transit infrastructure

The proposed improvements address these issues through:
- **EQUITY FIRST**: Southside and Clayton County get priority investment
- **ANTI-DISPLACEMENT**: Community land trusts and affordable housing protection
- **NO NEW HIGHWAYS** through residential neighborhoods
- **TRANSIT OVER HIGHWAYS**: Connecting communities instead of dividing them
- **COMMUNITY CONTROL**: Local boards with veto power over plans

This plan would transform Atlanta from a car-dependent, congested, inequitable metro into a balanced, multimodal region with world-class transit that serves ALL communities - especially those historically harmed by transportation policy.

**Timeline:** 20 years, with equity priorities in first 5 years
**Investment:** $40-51B (60% to underserved communities)
**Result:** Sustainable, equitable, efficient transportation system that repairs historic harms and prevents displacement
