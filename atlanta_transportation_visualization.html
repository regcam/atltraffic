Safety, Anti-Displacement & Crime Prevention Framework

🏗️
Station Safety by Design (CPTED)
  • Mandatory lighting standards on all platforms & access paths (specified in lux)
  • Sightline requirements — no blind corners, enclosed alcoves, or dead zones
  • Platform screen doors on all underground & elevated stations
  • Active ground-floor uses required at entrances (minimum 16 hrs/day)
  • Community-led pre-opening safety audits before every station launch
🚶
First & Last Mile Safety
  • Protected pedestrian crossings on all arterials within ¼ mile of every station
  • Continuous sidewalk connections to surrounding neighborhoods (not just the platform)
  • Protected bike lanes connecting stations to the street grid
  • Traffic calming on high-speed arterials adjacent to suburban extensions
  • Full lighting on all pedestrian access routes, not just station platforms
🏠
Enhanced Anti-Displacement
  • Pre-announcement Transit Land Acquisition Fund — buy parcels before routes are confirmed
  • Anti-speculation tax on vacant land near planned stations; revenue funds CLT acquisitions
  • Legally binding Community Benefit Agreements (CBAs) with financial penalties for non-compliance
  • Commercial rent stabilization & right of first refusal for legacy businesses (5+ yrs)
  • Displacement early-warning system tracking rent, evictions & bulk ownership transfers
🤝
Crime Prevention & Public Safety
  • Unarmed community ambassadors as primary station safety presence (de-escalation trained)
  • Civilian oversight board for MARTA police with subpoena power & majority-Black membership
  • Mental health outreach & social services co-located at Five Points, Airport, Murphy Crossing & Clayton County stations
  • Income-based fare caps: $1 max for riders below 200% of federal poverty line
  • Free transit for youth under 18 · Civil-only fare enforcement — no arrests, no criminal records

Practical Obstacles & Mitigations

Six categories of risk and the evidence-based strategies to address them

💰
Funding Fragility
  • Land Value Capture via TIF districts at every station — no federal approval needed
  • Congestion pricing on I-285 & Downtown Connector; revenue dedicated to MARTA
  • Phase funding asks to match results: unlock Phase 2 only after Phase 1 ridership targets are met
  • FTA New Starts program can fund 50–80% per segment if structured correctly from Year 1
🗳️
Political Fragmentation
  • Build employer coalitions in Gwinnett & Cobb (hospitals, corporations) — not county governments
  • Rebrand suburban service to remove “MARTA” political baggage in resistant counties
  • Target Gwinnett’s 52–48% loss with precision turnout among majority-minority voters
  • Georgia Chamber + Delta, Coca-Cola, Home Depot as state legislature advocates
⚙️
Implementation Sequencing
  • “Route blindness” land acquisition in general corridors before exact alignments are confirmed
  • Budget $50–75M in Phase 1 for anti-displacement infrastructure — CLT Fund, CBAs, early-warning system
  • Redesign bus network to feed new stations immediately ($10–20M, high ridership ROI)
  • Clayton County extension as the proof of concept that unlocks Phase 2 & 3 buy-in
🏛️
Institutional Change
  • Pilot community ambassadors at 2–3 stations for 18 months; scale on documented results
  • Distribute reduced-fare cards via SNAP/Medicaid enrollment — no gate verification needed
  • Transit workforce pipeline with Atlanta Technical College & HBCUs starting in Phase 1
  • Establish civilian oversight as advisory board first; build toward subpoena authority incrementally
🚗
Demand & Behavior
  • Require mixed-use development at every station for all-day ridership beyond peak commuting
  • Park-and-ride at suburban termini (Alpharetta, Gwinnett) to capture drive-to-transit riders early
  • I-285 peak tolls of $8–12 make transit at $2.50 genuinely competitive for suburban commuters
🤜
Opposition Management
  • Offer early CBA signers expedited permits, density bonuses & priority development rights
  • Narrow anti-speculation tax: exempt owner-occupied & small businesses; target corporate vacant lots only
  • Build 50+ org coalition (hospitals, faith, labor, business) before any public announcement
✊ Equity-First — No new highways through residential neighborhoods · Pre-announcement land acquisition · Community land trusts at every station · CBAs with developer penalties · CPTED design standards · Unarmed community ambassadors · Free transit for youth · 60% of Phase 1 funding to underserved communities
